TLS (Transport Layer Security) is a cryptographic protocol used to secure communications over a computer network. It encrypts data sent between clients and servers, ensuring privacy and integrity. TLS is commonly used for HTTPS (secure web browsing), email, and other network services.

TSP (Time Stamp Protocol) is a protocol defined by RFC 3161 that allows users to prove that a piece of data existed at a certain time. It is used in digital signatures and document authentication, providing trusted timestamps from a Time Stamping Authority (TSA).

LS: Secures network communication with encryption.
TSP: Provides trusted timestamps for data authentication.
